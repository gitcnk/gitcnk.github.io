2 P-values, Jelly-Beans and Mosquitos

The title of this chapter is rather strange. If you keep reading, you’ll see why I included the terms “Jelly Beans” and “Mosquitos”. The main goal of this chapter is to better understand the concept of the p-value, its limitations and misinterpretations. Let’s dive in.

2.1 What is a p-value?

The short answer is that it is a conditional probability. Then you might ask: “conditional on what?”. It is conditional on the null hypothesis. For example, suppose we are testing whether average heart rate of males is higher than that of females.

  • \(H_0:\ \mu_{male} = \mu_{female}\)

  • \(H_1:\ \mu_{male} > \mu_{female}\)

We collect data from two independent random samples. Say we have a sample mean difference of \(\bar x_{male} - \bar x_{female} = 13\) bpm (beats per minute). As good statisticians, first we ask the question: “Is this difference real or is it a result of chance?”. So we perform a two sample t test to answer this question. Suppose we got a p-value of .001. How do we interpret this?

Recall that the p-value is the probability of observing a sample mean difference of 13 (or more extreme) given the null hypothesis. That is:

\[ p-value = P[(\bar x_{M} - \bar x_{F} ) \geq 13 \ | \ \mu_{M} = \mu_{F}] \]

In simple terms, all this is saying is, how likely it is to observe a sample mean difference of 13 (or more) IF males and females have similar heart rates. This is where the troubles start. What is Type 1 error? Read section 4.3.3 of the intro stats book ( it is on Bb → Documents ) to understand this more.

Why do we usually pick p-val < 0.05 as a ‘rule’ to reject the null hypothesis? Do you agree with this ‘rule’?

The general form of most test statistic (like the t-statistic) is:
\[\frac{Estimate - Parameter } {Standard\ Error}\]

For example, the t-statistic has the following form:

\[T = \frac{\bar x - \mu }{\frac{s}{\sqrt n}}\]

where \(\mu\) is the population mean and \(s\) is the sample standard deviation. What will happen to the T statistic if you have a large sample? How does that affect the p-value of the test, would the p-value increase or decrease?
